## Japanese Business Card Ritual (Meishi)

### How to Give
1. Hold card with both hands
2. Face text toward recipient
3. Slight bow while giving
4. Say your name and title

### How to Receive
1. Accept with both hands
2. Study card carefully
3. Read name and title aloud
4. Place on table during meeting
5. Never write on it
6. Don't put in back pocket

### Card Placement
- On table during meeting (in order of seating)
- In card holder after meeting
- Never in back pocket (disrespectful)

## Regional Rules

### Japan
- Most formal business card culture
- Card = extension of person
- Treat with utmost respect
- Bring plenty of cards

### China
- Two hands to give/receive
- Study card carefully
- Don't write on it
- Place on table

### Korea
- Similar to Japan
- Two hands
- Respect hierarchy
- Age/status matters

### Middle East
- Right hand only
- Don't offer with left hand
- Study card carefully
- Don't write on it

### Western Countries
- More casual exchange
- One hand OK
- Can put in pocket
- Writing on it OK (notes)

## Business Card Etiquette

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Give with both hands (Asia) | Throw card away casually |
| Study card carefully | Write on card in front of giver |
| Place on table (Asia) | Put in back pocket (Japan) |
| Bring plenty of cards | Run out of cards |
| Use right hand (Muslim) | Use left hand (Muslim) |
| Say name/title (Japan) | Be casual about it |

## Safe Practices

- **Bring plenty of cards**
- **Use both hands** in Asia
- **Study card** before putting away
- **Don't write on it** in front of giver
- **Treat as extension** of person
