| Region | Punctuality | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Germany | Very punctual | Late = disrespectful |
| Japan | Very punctual | Early is better |
| Switzerland | Very punctual | Precise |
| USA | Punctual | 5 min early OK |
| UK | Punctual | On time expected |
| France | 5-10 min late OK | Fashionably late |
| Italy | 10-15 min late OK | Relaxed time |
| Spain | 10-15 min late OK | Relaxed |

| Brazil | 15-30 min late OK | Social events |

## Meeting Structure

### Japan
1. Formal introductions
2. Business card exchange
3. Small talk
4. Formal discussion
5. Decision may take time

### China
1. Relationship building first
2. Business card exchange
3. Tea/water offered
4. Discussion
5. Decision may need approval

### Germany
1. Punctual start
2. Formal introductions
3. Get to business quickly
4. Efficient discussion
5. Decision made in meeting

### USA
1. Casual introductions
2. Get to business
3. Direct discussion
4. Decision often in meeting
5. Follow-up actions

### Middle East
1. Tea/coffee offered
2. Small talk (relationship)
3. Business discussion
4. Decision takes time
5. Patience required

| USA | Less hierarchical |
| France | Moderate hierarchy |
| UK | Moderate hierarchy |

## Safe Practices

- **Arrive early** in Germany, Japan, Switzerland
- **Bring business cards** in Asia
- **Respect hierarchy** in Asia
- **Be patient** in Middle East, Latin America
- **Dress formally** in Asia, Middle East
