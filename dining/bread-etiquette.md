# 🍞 Bread Etiquette

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| France | Place bread on table, not plate | Low (if on plate) | Bread is "food companion" |
| Italy | Break bread, don't cut | Medium (if cutting) | Cutting is for formal |
| Germany | Don't put butter on whole slice | Low | Break piece, then butter |
| USA/UK | Bread on side plate | None | Butter at table |
| Middle East | Use bread as utensil | None | Tear with right hand |
| Japan | Bread eaten with hands | None | Chopsticks for rice |
| China | Baozi (bun) eaten with hands | None | Steamed buns |
| India | Roti/naan used to scoop | None | Right hand only |
| Russia | Bread is sacred | Medium (if wasted) | Never throw away |
| England | Don't butter whole slice | Low | Break and butter pieces |

## Key Rules

1. **Break, don't cut** (most cultures)
2. **Bread on table** (France, Italy)
3. **Use as utensil** (Middle East, India)
4. **Never waste** (Russia, religious contexts)
5. **Right hand** (Muslim/ Hindu cultures)

## Regional Variations

### France
- Bread goes directly on tablecloth
- Never on plate (except formal)
- Break with hands, never cut

### Italy
- Focaccia, ciabatta broken by hand
- Bread used to "fare la scarpetta" (sauce mop)
- Bread basket shared communally

### Middle East
- Pita/khubz torn with right hand
- Used to scoop hummus, baba ghanousj
- Never left uneaten

### India
- Roti, naan, chapati torn with right hand
- Used to scoop curries
- Always finish bread
