# 🍰 Dessert Customs

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| France | Cheese before dessert | Low (if skip) | Cheese course is traditional |
| Italy | No cappuccino after meal | Low (if ordered) | Milk drinks only for breakfast |
| USA | Dessert always offered | None | Sweet tooth culture |
| Japan | Red bean (anko) common | None | Less sweet than Western |
| China | Fruit or sweet soup | None | Not heavy Western desserts |
| India | Sweet after meal | None | Often milk-based |
| Mexico | Flan, tres leches | None | Sweet, creamy desserts |
| Turkey | Baklava, Turkish delight | None | Sweet, nutty treats |
| Germany | Black Forest cake | None | Rich, chocolate desserts |
| Middle East | Dates, baklava | None | Sweet, often with tea |

## Key Rules

1. **Cheese before dessert** (France, formal dining)
2. **No cappuccino after meal** (Italy, strong rule)
3. **Fruit common** in East Asia (lighter ending)
4. **Sweet tea/drink** in Middle East
5. **Always accept** when offered (shows appreciation)

## Regional Specialties

### France
- Cheese course first
- Then dessert (tarte, mousse)
- Petit fours with coffee

### Italy
- Tiramisu, panna cotta
- No cappuccino after lunch/dinner
- Espresso or digestivo

### Japan
- Wagashi (traditional sweets)
- Matcha with sweets
- Seasonal fruits

### Middle East
- Baklava, kunafa
- Turkish delight
- Dates with tea

### India
- Gulab jamun, kheer
- Lassi (sweet)
- Halwa

## Safe Practices

- **Accept dessert** when offered
- **Try local specialties**
- **Don't order cappuccino** after lunch in Italy
- **Cheese course** in France: try a little
