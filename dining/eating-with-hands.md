# 🍽️ Eating with Hands

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| India | Right hand only (left hand is unclean) | Extreme (if left hand) | Wash hands before and after |
| Ethiopia | Right hand only, from shared plate | Extreme (if left hand) | Use injera bread as utensil |
| Middle East | Right hand only | High (if left hand) | Left hand considered unclean |
| Southeast Asia | Right hand for solids | High (if left hand) | Similar to Middle East |
| Italy | Eat pizza with fork and knife | Medium (if用手) | Hands acceptable for casual |
| USA/UK | Fork and knife expected | Low (if用手) | Casual dining exceptions |
| Japan | Chopsticks expected | Low (if用手) | Considered impolite |
| China | Chopsticks expected | Low (if用手) | Chopsticks are standard |
| Africa (many) | Right hand from shared plate | High (if left hand) | Communal eating common |
| South America | Fork and knife expected | Low (if用手) | Formal settings |

## Key Rules

1. **Always use right hand** in India, Middle East, Africa, Southeast Asia
2. **Wash hands before and after** eating
3. **Use bread or injera** as utensil where traditional
4. **Observe locals** when unsure
5. **Never point with food on hands**

## Safe Practices

- Ask "How do you eat this?" when unsure
- Watch host's behavior
- Use utensils when available
- Keep left hand away from food
