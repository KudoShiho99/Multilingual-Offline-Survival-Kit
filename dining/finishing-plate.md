# 🍚 Finishing Your Plate

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| China | Finish everything (shows appreciation) | Medium (if you don't) | Host may keep refilling |
| Japan | Leave a little food (shows abundance) | Low (if you finish) | Leaving rice is exception |
| India | Finish everything (respect for food) | Medium (if you don't) | Waste is discouraged |
| USA/UK | Finish what you take | Low (if you don't) | Shows good manners |
| France | Finish your plate | Low (if you don't) | Waste is frowned upon |
| Middle East | Finish to show appreciation | Medium (if you don't) | Host will offer more |
| Thailand | Leave a little (shows host provided enough) | Low (if you finish) | Similar to Japan |
| Philippines | Finish everything | Medium (if you don't) | Waste is disrespectful |
| Russia | Finish to show respect | Medium (if you don't) | Waste is bad |
| Mexico | Finish everything | Low (if you don't) | Shows appreciation |

## Why It Varies

- **Finish everything**: Waste is disrespectful, shows appreciation
- **Leave a little**: Shows host provided abundantly, you're satisfied
- **Rice exception**: In Japan/Asia, rice is sacred — always finish rice

## Safe Practices

- **Observe host's behavior** first
- **Ask about rice**: If rice is served, finish it
- **Take small portions** to avoid waste
- **Say "delicious"** to show appreciation regardless

## Key Insight

- Rice cultures (Japan, Thailand): Leave a little
- Non-rice cultures (China, India, West): Finish everything
- When in doubt, finish — it's safer
