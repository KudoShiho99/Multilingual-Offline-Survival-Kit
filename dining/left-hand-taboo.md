# 🚫 Left Hand Taboo

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| India | Never use left hand for food or giving | Extreme | Left hand is "unclean" |
| Middle East | Left hand considered unclean | Extreme | Use right hand for eating/giving |
| Africa (many) | Left hand taboo | Extreme | Same as India/Middle East |
| Southeast Asia | Left hand avoided | High | Especially Thailand, Indonesia |
| Japan | No strong taboo | Low | Chopsticks make hand irrelevant |
| USA/UK | No taboo | None | Left-handed people eat normally |
| Europe (general) | No taboo | None | Left-handed utensils available |

## Why?

- Left hand traditionally used for personal hygiene
- Religious and cultural significance across many regions
- Deeply ingrained from childhood

## What to Do

- **Use right hand** for eating, passing food, greeting
- **If left-handed**, practice using right hand for meals
- **Carry wet wipes** to clean hands before eating
- **Observe locals** and follow their lead

## Exceptions

- In Western countries, left hand is perfectly acceptable
- When using utensils (fork, chopsticks), hand dominance matters less
- Modern urban areas may be more relaxed
