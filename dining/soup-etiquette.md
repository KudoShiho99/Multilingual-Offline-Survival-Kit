# 🍲 Soup Etiquette

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| Japan | Slurp loudly | None | Shows enjoyment, cools soup |
| China | Slurp is OK | Low | Acceptable in casual settings |
| UK | Slurp is rude | Medium | Quiet sipping expected |
| France | Slurp is rude | High | Very formal table manners |
| USA | Slurp is rude | Medium | Quiet eating expected |
| Korea | Slurp is acceptable | Low | Especially for hot soup |
| Thailand | Slurp is OK | Low | Common for noodle soups |
| Italy | Slurp is rude | Medium | Quiet eating expected |
| Russia | Slurp is OK | Low | Especially for hot borscht |
| Middle East | Slurp is rude | Medium | Formal dining rules |

## Why Slurping Varies

- **Cooling hot soup** (Japan, Korea, Russia)
- **Showing appreciation** (Japan, Korea)
- **Formal vs casual** (France formal, Japan casual)
- **Noodle soups** (Thailand, Japan, Korea allow slurping)

## How to Eat Soup

### East Asia
- Slurp is acceptable
- Lift bowl to mouth
- Use chopsticks for noodles
- Spoon for broth

### Western Countries
- Sip quietly
- Spoon away from you
- Don't lift bowl
- Knife and fork for chunks

## Key Rules

1. **Lift bowl** (East Asia) vs **keep on table** (West)
2. **Slurp OK** (Japan, Korea, Thailand) vs **rude** (UK, France, USA)
3. **Chopsticks** for noodles (Asia) vs **spoon only** (West)
4. **Finish soup** (shows appreciation)
5. **Don't blow on soup** (wait for it to cool)

## Safe Practices

- **Observe locals** first
- **When in doubt**, sip quietly
- **Lift bowl** in East Asia
- **Finish soup** to show appreciation
