# 🍵 Tea Etiquette

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| China | Receive cup with two hands | Medium (if one hand) | Respect for host |
| Japan | Slurp tea is polite | None | Shows appreciation |
| UK | Stir clockwise, don't clink | Low | Pinky down |
| India | Chai is social bonding | Low | Accept when offered |
| Turkey | Refusing tea is rude | Medium | Tea is hospitality |
| Morocco | Mint tea poured from height | None | Shows skill |
| Russia | Tea with sugar, jam | None | Strong black tea |
| Morocco | Tea with three glasses minimum | Medium | Symbol of friendship |

## Key Rules

1. **Accept tea when offered** (Middle East, Turkey, Morocco)
2. **Receive with two hands** (China, Japan, Korea)
3. **Don't refuse** (social bonding in many cultures)
4. **Slurping is OK** in Japan (shows enjoyment)
5. **Stir gently** in UK (don't splash)

## Regional Customs

### China
- Host pours for guests
- Receive cup with both hands
- Tap table to say "thank you"
- Tea refilled automatically

### Japan
- Slurp to show enjoyment
- Say "oishii" (delicious)
- Don't pour for yourself
- Wait for host to pour

### UK
- Milk goes in after tea
- Stir gently, no clinking
- Pinky down (no pinky up)
- Cup and saucer together

### Turkey
- Accept first glass
- Say "çay" (tea)
- Sugar cube optional
- Refusing is impolite

### Morocco
- Three glasses minimum
- Poured from height
- Mint + sugar traditional
- Refusing is rude

### India
- Chai is everywhere
- Accept when offered
- Sweet, milky, spiced
- Social bonding ritual

## Safe Phrases

- "Thank you, it's delicious"
- "Yes please, I'd love some"
- "This is wonderful"
- "You're too kind"
