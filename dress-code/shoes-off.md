## Shoe Removal Etiquette

### How to Remove
- Remove shoes at entrance
- Place shoes neatly
- Don't step on tatami with shoes
- Use slippers if provided

### What to Do with Feet
- Use provided slippers
- Don't go barefoot (impolite)
- Keep socks clean
- Toilet slippers separate (Japan)

### After Removing
- Don't cross legs (showing soles)
- Don't point feet at people
- Sit properly

## Regional Variations

### Japan
- Most strict about shoe removal
- Toilet slippers separate
- Tatami rooms require bare feet
- Never wear shoes on tatami

### Korea
- Remove shoes at entrance
- Oncheon (bathhouse) culture
- Home slippers provided
- Keep socks clean

### Middle East
- Remove before mosque prayer
- Right foot first into mosque
- Wudu (ablution) before prayer
- Respectful behavior

### Finland
- Sauna culture (always naked)
- Remove shoes at door
- Slippers provided
- Clean socks expected

## Safe Practices

- **Always remove** in Asia, Middle East
- **Look for shoe rack** at entrance
- **Use slippers** if provided
- **Keep socks clean**
- **When in doubt**, remove shoes
