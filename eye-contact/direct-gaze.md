## Why It Varies

### Direct eye contact cultures (West)
- Shows honesty, confidence, engagement
- Avoiding = suspicious, dishonest
- Expected in business, social settings

### Indirect eye contact cultures (East)
- Shows respect, deference
- Direct = challenging, rude
- Especially with elders, superiors

## Eye Contact Rules

| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Greeting | Brief eye contact, then look away |
| Listening | Moderate eye contact |
| Speaking | More eye contact |
| With elders | Avert eyes (Asia) |
| With superiors | Avert eyes (Asia, Africa) |
| Opposite gender | Avoid in conservative areas |
| Business | Direct in West, avoid in East |

## How Much Eye Contact

| Culture | Appropriate Duration |
|---------|---------------------|
| Western | 3-5 seconds, then break |
| East Asian | Brief, then look away |
| Middle East | Same gender: sustained; Opposite: brief |
| Latin America | Moderate, warm |

## Safe Practices

- **Observe locals** first
- **Match their eye contact level**
- **Don't stare** (any culture)
- **Break eye contact** periodically
- **When in doubt**, look at nose/forehead
