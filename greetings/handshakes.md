# 🤝 Handshake Customs

| Region | Custom | Offense Level | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------|
| USA/UK | Firm handshake, eye contact | Low (weak grip) | Shows confidence |
| Germany | Very firm, brief | Low (weak grip) | Strong = trustworthy |
| Japan | Light, bow while shaking | Low (too firm) | Respect hierarchy |
| China | Light, may be prolonged | Low | Don't squeeze hard |
| France | Light, brief | None | Quick, not firm |
| Italy | Warm, may be 2-3 times | None | Friendly approach |
| Spain | Two hands (left on forearm) | None | Warm, personal |
| Middle East | Right hand only, light | High (left hand) | Left hand is unclean |
| India | Right hand only | High (left hand) | Namaste alternative |
| Russia | Very firm, direct eye contact | Low (weak) | Shows strength |
| Brazil | Warm, may include hug | None | Friendly, personal |
| Mexico | Warm, may include pat on back | None | Friendly |
| Turkey | Right hand, may kiss knuckles | None | For elders |
| Thailand | Wai (prayer hands) instead | Low (handshake) | Handshake acceptable for foreigners |
| Korea | Light, slight bow | Low | Respect for elders |
| Australia | Firm, casual | None | Friendly |
| South Africa | Firm, may be prolonged | None | Shows trust |
| Nigeria | May be very long | None | Don't rush |
| Ethiopia | Light, prolonged | None | Respectful |
| Egypt | Right hand, may touch heart after | None | Sincerity |

## Key Rules

1. **Always right hand** (Muslim, Hindu, African cultures)
2. **Firm in West** (USA, Germany, Russia)
3. **Light in East** (Japan, China, Korea)
4. **Bow in Asia** (Japan, Korea, Thailand)
5. **Eye contact varies** (direct in West, avoid in Asia)

## Alternatives

- 🙏 **Namaste** (India, Nepal) - palms together, slight bow
- 🙇 **Bow** (Japan, Korea) - angle depends on status
- 🤲 **Wai** (Thailand) - prayer hands with bow
- 🫂 **Hug** (Latin America, close friends)
- 💋 **Cheek kiss** (France, Middle East, Latin America)

## When to Avoid Handshake

- Opposite gender in conservative Muslim areas
- When person has cold/illness
- During COVID-19 pandemic
- When hands are dirty (eating, working)
